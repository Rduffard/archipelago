# Reputation Model

This is a first-pass social consequence framework for Archipelago.

It is intentionally broader than island politics. The goal is to track how major power blocs read a character before the campaign starts, without forcing the sheet to store dozens of tiny factions on day one.

## Core Tracks

- `Yuma Republic`: officers, western state power, industrial militarists, state inventors.
- `Lilin Empire`: eastern ritual power, arcanists, spirit courts, imperial mystics.
- `Free Captains`: pirates, rogue sailors, anti-imperial crews, Pirate King loyalists.
- `Rebel Movements`: insurgents, labor agitators, revolutionaries, anti-occupation cells.
- `Guild Consortium`: shipwrights, artificers, traders, industrial craft elites.
- `Noble Courts`: duchies, feudal houses, court officials, legitimacy-driven rulers.
- `Underworld Networks`: smugglers, assassins, spies, black market operators.
- `Faith Orders`: monasteries, shrine keepers, sacred institutions, spiritual orders.
- `Sea Peoples`: fishfolk nations, coralborn communities, tide mystics, submerged powers.
- `Frontier Tribes`: Akshan tribes, wildland guardians, non-imperial ancestral cultures.

## Score Scale

- `-3 Hated`: doors close, social checks often begin at disadvantage, enemies act first.
- `-2 Distrusted`: suspicion, extra scrutiny, worse prices, demands for proof.
- `-1 Wary`: guarded interactions, no automatic trust, slower access.
- `0 Unknown`: neutral starting state.
- `+1 Known Favorably`: smoother introductions, small courtesies, occasional advantage.
- `+2 Trusted`: meaningful support, easier access, allies take reasonable risks for you.
- `+3 Champion`: insider status, symbolic importance, active protection or backing.

## Why These Tracks

The lore suggests three overlapping kinds of reputation:

- imperial alignment
- social class / institutional alignment
- cultural alignment

These ten tracks cover those layers without making each island a separate meter.

That means:

- `Yuma`, `Lilin`, and later campaign choices can swing the imperial tracks hard.
- islands like `Klechi`, `Tetlanco`, and `Arannia` naturally feed into guild, noble, and underworld tracks.
- islands like `Thult`, `Rukka`, `Kirkia`, and `Akshan` feed into culture-facing tracks that matter outside imperial politics.

## Origin Examples

- `Yuma` starts high with `Yuma Republic`, a little high with `Guild Consortium`, and negative with `Lilin Empire`, `Free Captains`, and `Rebel Movements`.
- `Yayimi` starts strongly anti-Yuma and positively aligned with pirates and resistance.
- `Klechi` starts high with `Guild Consortium` and slightly positive with `Free Captains` and `Underworld Networks`.
- `Tetlanco` starts strong with `Noble Courts`, a little positive with `Underworld Networks`, and slightly negative with pirates and rebels.
- `Purapet` starts high with `Faith Orders`.
- `Thult` and `Rukka` start strongly positive with `Sea Peoples`.
- `Akshan` starts strongly positive with `Frontier Tribes` and somewhat positive with `Rebel Movements`.

## Suggested Mechanical Uses

- `+1`: advantage on first impressions, introductions, or basic favors once per scene.
- `+2`: access to faction gear, sanctuary, informants, or reduced prices.
- `+3`: faction missions, elite backing, titles, escorts, relic access, or political leverage.
- `-1`: guarded NPC stance, higher DCs, or reduced information.
- `-2`: restricted access, worse deals, more checkpoints, shadowing, or refused aid.
- `-3`: automatic escalation, ambush risk, denial of service, or faction-hostile complications.

## Future Expansion

Later, if we want more granularity, broad tracks can branch into subfactions:

- `Free Captains` could branch into named flotillas and successor claimants.
- `Noble Courts` could branch into `Tetlanco`, `Sha-Ni`, `Dazibinian`, and `Silvia`.
- `Sea Peoples` could branch into `Thult`, `Rukka`, and neutral coralborn interests.
- `Faith Orders` could split sacred institutions from occult or relic-focused sects.

The current model is meant to be the campaign-safe baseline, not the final political simulation.
